# Voice

This file holds your project's voice — the way you want the writer to sound. Fill it in once at install time; the writer (and editor) load it before drafting.

The agents will treat any section that still reads like placeholder prose as unfilled. Replace the prose below with your project's actual voice, or delete the section entirely if it doesn't apply.

## Tone

> *Placeholder.* Describe the tone the writer should aim for. Examples:
>
> - *Confident but not arrogant. Direct without being curt. Warm without being chummy.*
> - *Technical and precise. No hedging. Comfortable with jargon the reader will know.*
> - *Conversational. First person plural. Like one engineer explaining something to another over coffee.*

## Person and POV

> *Placeholder.* Name the point of view you write from.
>
> - *Second person (you) for instructional content; first person plural (we) for company/team posts.*
> - *Third person for case studies. Always name the protagonist.*
> - *Never first person singular (I).*

## Vocabulary

> *Placeholder.* Words and phrases your project uses, with their preferred form.
>
> - *Customers, not users.*
> - *Ship, not release. Cut, not remove. Land, not merge.*
> - *Always "the team", never "the org".*

## What we don't sound like

> *Placeholder.* The anti-patterns. Words and shapes the writer should refuse.
>
> - *Corporate-marketing speak: "leverage", "synergy", "going forward", "at the end of the day".*
> - *Buzzwords: "AI-powered", "next-generation", "best-in-class".*
> - *Hedging: "may", "might", "could potentially", "perhaps".*
> - *Generic transitions: "Furthermore", "Moreover", "Additionally".*
